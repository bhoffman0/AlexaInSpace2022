    <p class="overview">
    This unit focuses on <b>animation</b>, <b>simulation</b> and <b>modeling</b>. The <i>LightsOff</i> app introduces you to the idea of computer simulation with a computational variation of the traditional Whack-a-Mole game. <i>The Coin Flip</i> app, which extends over several lessons, will introduce you to the concept of modeling. The activities in this unit build toward understanding how programmers use abstractions, such as a <span class="hover vocab yui-wk-div" data-id='PRNG'>pseudorandom number generator (PRNG)</span>, to represent real world situations in programs, in this case, the flipping of a coin. You will learn how <span class="hover vocab yui-wk-div" data-id='PRNG'>PRNG</span> algorithms are used to <span class="hover vocab yui-wk-div" data-id='model'>model</span> <span class="hover vocab yui-wk-div" data-id='random'>randomness</span> inside a computer, such as with the <i>Coin Flip</i> app. Then, you'll extend the app <span class="hover vocab yui-wk-div" data-id='model'>model</span> to represent different types of coins, including a biased coin and a three-­sided coin. This is followed by an experiment lesson where an app that repeatedly “flips” a coin is used to assess the quality of App Inventor’s <span class="hover vocab yui-wk-div" data-id='PRNG'>PRNG</span>; At the end of the unit, you'll learn how one’s privacy is impacted by developing technology and computing innovations as well as learn and explore the economic, social and cultural effects of computing innovations, such as real world models of the weather and the solar system. 
    </p>
